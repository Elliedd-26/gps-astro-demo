Last Wednesday at The Garrison, more than forty Chinese-speaking queer women gathered for our monthly Traffic Light Social — and what started as a small experiment in color-coded stickers turned into one of the warmest nights we've had this year.

The premise was simple. A red sticker meant *"just here to listen."* Yellow meant *"open to chat."* Green meant *"looking to connect."* Within an hour, the room had organized itself into easy clusters — greens leaning in by the bar, yellows drifting around the snack table, reds finding quiet corners — but everyone, somehow, in the same room together.

What we keep hearing afterward is that it was the language. Bilingual conversations slipped between Mandarin, Cantonese, and English without anyone keeping score. Two strangers exchanged WeChat IDs by the coat check. A first-timer told us she'd been looking for a queer Chinese-speaking space in Toronto for two years.

We had been looking for her, too. See you in June.

— *GPS Toronto*

上周三在 The Garrison，四十多位讲中文的酷儿女性聚在一起，参加我们每月一次的「红绿灯社交之夜」。原本只是一个小小的实验——用三种颜色的贴纸把见面这件事变得轻一点——没想到成了今年最温暖的一个晚上。

规则很简单。红色：*"我只是来听一听。"* 黄色：*"可以聊一聊。"* 绿色：*"想认识新朋友。"* 一个小时之内，房间自己分成了几块——绿色的姐妹们围在吧台聊得起劲，黄色的人在零食桌附近转来转去，红色的人找到了安静的角落——但所有人，又都在同一个空间里。

事后我们听到最多的一句话是：因为是中文。普通话、广东话、英文随便切换，没人在意；新认识的两个人在衣帽间换了微信号；一个第一次来的姐妹说，她在多伦多找一个讲中文的酷儿空间，找了两年。

其实，我们也找了她两年。六月再见。

—— *GPS Toronto*
