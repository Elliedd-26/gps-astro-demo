Last Friday at Sam James on Harbord, fifteen of us pulled up chairs and tried something we'd been talking about for months: speed coffee chats, but for careers.

Five mentors — a UX designer at a fintech, a corporate lawyer, a podcaster who runs a Chinese-language show, a non-profit director, and a senior engineer at Shopify — sat at five tables. The rest of us rotated every fifteen minutes. By the end of the night everyone had heard five completely different versions of what *"building a career as a Chinese-speaking woman in Toronto"* can actually look like.

A few things we keep thinking about:

- How often the first job after immigration doesn't fit — and how that's okay.
- How being queer at work is something most of us have negotiated case-by-case, not once-and-for-all.
- How a good mentor doesn't always look like a senior version of you.

Same time next month — bring a friend who's job-searching.

— *GPS Toronto*

上周五在 Harbord 街的 Sam James 咖啡馆，十五个人围坐下来，试了一件我们讨论了好几个月的事：职场版的速配咖啡聊。

五位 mentor——一位 fintech 公司的 UX 设计师，一位企业法务，一位主理中文播客的姐妹，一位非盈利机构总监，一位 Shopify 的高级工程师——分别坐在五张桌子前。剩下的人每十五分钟换一桌。一晚上下来，每个人都听到了五个完全不同的版本——*「在多伦多，作为一个讲中文的女性，建立自己的事业」*可以长什么样。

有几句话一直在脑子里转：

- 移民后的第一份工作常常不合适——没关系。
- 在职场作为 queer，大多数人都是一件一件地谈，而不是一次想清楚。
- 好的 mentor 不一定长得像「年长版的你」。

下个月同一时间——记得带上一个正在找工作的朋友。

—— *GPS Toronto*
